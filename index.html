Neil Patel

WSSH Specialist @ DAI

About Me

I’m a water security, sanitation, and hygiene specialist at DAI supporting a portfolio of USAID-funded WSSH programs around the world, primarily the USAID/Philippines Safe Water and USAID/Haiti Eau activities. I additionally provide short-term technical assistance as a WSSH specialist to a variety of water, biodiversity, and climate adaptation programs, including:  USAID Africa Trade & Investment, USAID Fragile Waters, and UK Global Centre on Biodiversity on Climate.

Prior to this role, I was the Project Analyst for USAID’s Water, Sanitation, and Hygiene Finance (WASH-FIN) 2, where I led data analytics, monitoring, and impact evaluation efforts for a $73M program operating in ten countries (with primary operations in India, Kenya, Ghana, and Rwanda) to mobilize financing for climate resilient water and sanitation services. I also previously worked on USAID’s Water, Sanitation, and Hygiene Finance (WASH-FIN) and Uganda Sanitation for Health Activity (USHA) activities.

I have a master’s degree in Data, Economics, and Development Policy from MIT, and an undergraduate degree in Business Honors & Plan II from the University of Texas at Austin.

Research

My research primarily focuses on the use of geospatial AI to improve water security and access in urban areas. I was a research affiliate with the MIT Senseable City Lab and MIT D-Lab, working on projects ranging from designing a sensor system to monitor vermifilter wastewater treatment systems in rural Colombia to developing deep learning models trained to detect and geo-reference water points using street-level imagery in Lagos. You can learn more about my projects on this page.

Groundwater Youth Network

I’m passionate about youth representation in the water sector and represent North America on the global steering committee for UNESCO’s Groundwater Youth Network, supporting research, career development, and capacity-building programs for a body of 1,400+ young water professionals representing 114 countries. I have advocated for youth inclusion in the Sustainable Development Goals at the United Nations’ General Assembly and the High-Level Political Forum for Sustainable Development.
